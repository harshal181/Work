 Q.1.Retrieve the names of all customers from the `Customers` table, sorted in alphabetical order.
Ans:-select name from customer
     order by name;

Q.2. Fetch the top 10 most expensive products from the `Products` table. 
Ans:-select product_name 
    from products
    order by price desc
    limit 10;

Q.3. Write a query to get the second-highest salary from the `Employees` table. 
Ans:- Select salary from Employee
      order by salary desc
      limit 1,1;

Q.4. Get the total count of customers from each city in the `Customers` table. 
Ans:- select city ,count(*)
     from customer
    group by city;
Q.5. Retrieve department-wise total salary from the `Employees` table but only include 
departments where the total salary is greater than 2,00,000.
Ans:- select depart_name,salary,count(*)
      from department
      where salary>2,00,000
      group by department_name;

Q.6. Extract only the last four characters of the `phone_number` column and first three characters 
from `customerName` columns from the `Customers` table.
Ans:- select phone_number,customer_name 
      from customer
      where phone_number like"%----" customer_name like'---%";

 Q7. Write a query to retrieve products where the `product_name` contains "Pro". 
Ans:-select product_name
     from product
     where product_name like '%Pro%';

Q.8. Fetch the total number of students per course from the `Students` table, only for courses that 
have at least 30 students enrolled. 
Ans:- select course_name,count(*)
      from student
      group by course_name
      having count(*)>=30;
Q.9. Write an SQL query to find the number of orders placed in each month of the year. 
Ans:-select month(order_date) AS order_month, count(*) as total_orders
     from `order`
      group by month(order_date)
      order by(order_date);
Q.10. Get the highest and lowest salary per department from the `Employees` table.
Ans:-select max(salary),min (salary)
     from Employees
     group by did;
